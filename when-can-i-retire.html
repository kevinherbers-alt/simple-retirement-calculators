How the projection works

Each year, your balance grows by your expected return and your annual contribution is added. The calculator repeats this until your balance crosses your target number, then reports the year and your age when it happens. The chart splits your balance into money you contributed and growth from compounding — over long timelines, growth usually ends up doing most of the work.

Choosing your inputs

Not sure what target to use? Find it first with the retirement number calculator — for most people it's roughly 25× the annual income their portfolio needs to produce. For the return assumption, the U.S. stock market's long-run average has been around 7% after inflation, but using a range (say 5%–8%) will show you how sensitive your retirement date is to market performance. If you want to see whether your current savings alone could get you there with no further contributions, that's the Coast FIRE calculator.

This tool is for educational purposes only and is not financial advice. Projections assume a constant annual return, which real markets never deliver, and do not account for taxes, fees, or inflation unless you use inflation-adjusted inputs. Consult a qualified financial professional before making retirement decisions.
