How your retirement number is calculated

The math is simple: take the annual income your portfolio needs to produce (your desired income minus Social Security, pensions, or other income), and divide by your withdrawal rate. At the classic 4% rate, that works out to 25× your annual portfolio income need. Want $40,000 a year from your portfolio? Your number is $1,000,000.

The withdrawal rate is the big assumption. The 4% rule comes from research on how much retirees could withdraw annually (adjusted for inflation) from a diversified portfolio without running out of money over 30 years. A lower rate like 3% builds in more safety and suits earlier retirements; a higher rate like 5% assumes flexibility or a shorter retirement.
