How the drawdown projection works

Each year, your balance grows by your expected return, then your annual withdrawal is taken out. If you set an inflation adjustment, the withdrawal increases by that percentage every year to preserve your purchasing power — which is how the original 4% rule research modeled spending. The calculator runs until the balance hits zero or 60 years pass, whichever comes first.

If your return outpaces your inflation-adjusted withdrawals, the balance can grow forever — the calculator will tell you if your plan is self-sustaining. A common benchmark: at a 4% initial withdrawal rate with a balanced portfolio, savings have historically lasted at least 30 years. See the 4% rule explained for the background.
