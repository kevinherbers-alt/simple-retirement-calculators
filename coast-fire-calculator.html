How the Coast FIRE number is calculated

Your Coast FIRE number is your retirement target discounted back to today at your expected return: target ÷ (1 + return)years until retirement. If your current savings meet or exceed that amount, compounding alone should carry you to your goal — you could stop contributing and just cover your living expenses until retirement.

Reaching Coast FIRE doesn't mean you should stop saving — it means you've bought flexibility. A lower-stress job, a career change, or a sabbatical becomes financially survivable because your retirement is already "handled" by your existing portfolio.
