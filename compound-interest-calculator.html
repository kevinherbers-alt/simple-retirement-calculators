How compound interest works

Compounding means you earn returns not just on the money you put in, but on the returns themselves. In year one, growth comes mostly from your contributions. But each year's gains join the balance and start earning their own gains — and over decades, that snowball usually ends up larger than everything you contributed. That crossover, visible in the chart above, is why starting early matters more than starting big.

This calculator compounds monthly, which matches how most investment and savings accounts grow in practice, and applies contributions at the end of each period.

Using it for retirement planning

This is the engine behind retirement projections. To point it at a specific goal, use the when can I retire calculator, which runs the same math against a target number, or find that target first with the retirement number calculator.
